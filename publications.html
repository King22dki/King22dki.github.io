Master's Thesis

Development of Group Contribution Models for Furanic Compounds
Daniel King Igwe
Imperial College London, September 2020 | Funding: £3.5 million
Abstract: This thesis presents the development of novel group contribution models for predicting thermodynamic properties of furanic compounds using Statistical Associating Fluid Theory (SAFT) Equation of State. Furanic compounds, derived from biomass, are increasingly important in sustainable chemical processes. The research utilized advanced computational methods and Python-based data analysis (Matplotlib, Pandas, scikit-learn, NumPy) to model fluid phase equilibria. The developed models enable accurate prediction of properties crucial for process design in bio-refineries and sustainable chemical manufacturing. Database integration involved converting and optimizing data formats (CSV to XML and JSON) for efficient computation and validation against experimental measurements.

Air Pollution Dispersion Modeling Using Computational Fluid Dynamics
Daniel King Igwe
London South Bank University Research Symposium, 2019
Presented research on comprehensive air pollution dispersion modeling using Star CCM+ to predict complex air flows around seven high-rise buildings in London. Investigated integration of deep learning with CFD for adaptive meshing strategies to improve computational efficiency and accuracy in urban environmental modeling.
