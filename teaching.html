Teaching Philosophy

Learning as Discovery

I believe that effective teaching goes beyond mere transmission of information—it involves cultivating genuine curiosity, developing critical thinking skills, and empowering students to become independent learners. Mathematics and engineering are not collections of formulas to memorize but ways of thinking about the world. My approach emphasizes understanding over memorization, application over abstraction, and connections between disciplines over isolated facts.

As an autodidact myself, I understand the challenges and rewards of self-directed learning. I strive to create environments where students feel comfortable asking questions, making mistakes, and exploring ideas deeply. Technology, particularly AI tools, offers tremendous potential to personalize learning and make education more accessible—when used thoughtfully and ethically.
